<p>The <strong><code>ellipse()</code></strong> <a href="/en-US/docs/Web/CSS">CSS</a> function is one of the {{cssxref("&lt;basic-shape&gt;")}} <a href="/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/CSS_Types">data types</a>.</p>

<h2 id="Syntax">Syntax</h2>

<pre class="brush: css">shape-outside: ellipse(40% 50% at left);
shape-outside: ellipse(closest-side farthest-side at 30%);
</pre>

<p>An ellipse is essentially a squashed circle and so <code>ellipse()</code> acts in a very similar way to {{cssxref("basic-shape/circle()","circle()")}} except that we have to specify two radii x and y.</p>

<h3>Values</h3>

<dl>
  <dt><code>&lt;shape-radius&gt;</code></dt>
  <dd><p>Two radii, x and y in that order. These may be a {{cssxref("length")}}, or a {{cssxref("percentage")}} or values <code>closest-side</code> and <code>farthest-side</code>.</p>
    <dl>
      <dt><code>closest-side</code></dt>
      <dd>Uses the length from the center of the shape to the closest side of the reference box. For ellipses, this is the closest side in the radius dimension.</dd>
      <dt><code>farthest-side</code></dt>
      <dd>Uses the length from the center of the shape to the farthest side of the reference box. For ellipses, this is the farthest side in the radius dimension.</dd>
    </dl>
  </dd>
  <dt><code>&lt;position&gt;</code></dt>
  <dd>Moves the center of the ellipse. May be a {{cssxref("length")}}, or a {{cssxref("percentage")}}, or a values such as <code>left</code>.</dd>
</dl>

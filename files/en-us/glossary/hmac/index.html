HMAC is a protocol used for {{Glossary("cryptography", "cryptographically")}} authenticating messages. It can use any kind of {{Glossary("Cryptographic hash function", "cryptographic functions")}}, and its strength depends on the underlying function (SHA1 or MD5 for instance), and the chosen secret key. With such a combination, the HMAC verification {{Glossary("Algorithm", "algorithm")}} is then known with a compound name such as HMAC-SHA1.

HMAC is used to ensure both integrity and authentication.

<p><strong>Whitespace</strong> refers to {{Glossary("Character", "characters")}} which are used to provide horizontal or vertical space between other characters. Whitespace is often used to separate tokens in {{Glossary("HTML")}}, {{Glossary("CSS")}}, {{Glossary("JavaScript")}}, and other computer languages.</p>

<p>Whitespace characters and their usage vary among languages.</p>

<h2 id="In_HTML">In HTML</h2>

<p>The <a href="https://infra.spec.whatwg.org/#ascii-whitespace">Infra Living Standard</a> defines five characters as “ASCII whitespace”: U+0009 TAB, U+000A LF, U+000C FF, U+000D CR, and U+0020 SPACE.</p>

<h2 id="In_JavaScript">In JavaScript</h2>

<p>The <a href="https://tc39.es/ecma262/#sec-white-space">ECMAScript Language Specification</a> defines several Unicode codepoints as “white space”: U+0009 CHARACTER TABULATION &lt;TAB&gt;, U+000B LINE TABULATION &lt;VT&gt;, U+000C FORM FEED &lt;FF&gt;, U+0020 SPACE &lt;SP&gt;, U+00A0 NO-BREAK SPACE &lt;NBSP&gt;, U+FEFF ZERO WIDTH NO-BREAK SPACE &lt;ZWNBSP&gt;, and any other Unicode “Space_Separator” code points &lt;USP&gt;.</p>

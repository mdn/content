LTR (Left To Right) is a {{Glossary("locale")}} property indicatingÂ that text is written from left to right. For example, the en-US locale (for US English) specifies left-to-right. Most Western languages, as well as many others around the world, are written LTR.

The opposite of LTR, {{Glossary("RTL")}} (Right To Left) is used in other common languages, including Arabic (ar) and Hebrew (he).
